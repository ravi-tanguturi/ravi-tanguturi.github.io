Enterprises Should Augment Microsoft Purview and Agent 365 With Independent AI TRiSM Controls

Microsoft Ignite 2025 · 10 min read

At Microsoft Ignite 2025, Microsoft made a decisive statement: AI agents are no longer experimental tools — they are enterprise actors that require identity, governance, and security controls.

With major announcements across Agent 365, Entra Agent ID, expanded Microsoft Purview, and AI-assisted security operations, Microsoft is constructing a native control plane for AI agents operating across Microsoft 365, Copilot, Fabric, and Foundry.

This is a meaningful and necessary evolution. However, as enterprises deploy AI across multiple clouds, data platforms, and third-party tools, a critical reality emerges:

Native, vendor-scoped controls alone are not sufficient for enterprise-wide AI trust, risk, and security management.

What Microsoft Got Right

Agent 365: Centralized Oversight for AI Agents

Agent 365 introduces a centralized lifecycle and management plane for AI agents, treating them as digital employees rather than disposable tools.

  • Agent registration and inventory
  • Lifecycle and access management
  • Integration with Entra, Defender, Purview, and Sentinel
  • Governance embedded into agent build workflows

Embedding governance at the point of agent creation aligns operational controls with developer workflows rather than retrofitting security after deployment.

Entra Agent ID: Identity for Non-Human Actors

Entra Agent ID extends Zero Trust identity concepts to AI agents by enabling unique identities, authentication, authorization, and policy enforcement.

Identity is foundational. Without it, governance cannot scale.

Expanded Microsoft Purview for AI

Microsoft Purview remains a core pillar of AI data governance, offering:

  • Real-time DLP for Copilot prompts and responses
  • DSPM-driven understanding of data exposure
  • Integration with Insider Risk Management
  • AI-assisted investigation and triage

Where the Gaps Remain

Deep Protections Depend on Entra Registration

The most advanced auditing and enforcement capabilities apply only to agents registered with Entra Agent ID.

  • Registration is opt-in
  • Unregistered agents receive limited visibility
  • Shadow and rogue agents can operate with reduced oversight

Key Preventative Controls Remain in Preview

Several critical AI security capabilities remain in Preview, including:

  • Shadow AI agent detection
  • TLS traffic inspection
  • Network-layer prompt injection protection

Understanding the Insider Threat Vectors

Global Secure Access (GSA) Client Bypass

The GSA client enforces Zero Trust access and inspection on endpoints. A malicious insider may disable or bypass the client, allowing AI tools to operate outside inspection on unmanaged devices.

Shadow AI Agents

Shadow AI agents operate outside IT governance, often embedded in scripts, browser extensions, or automation tools.

Until detection reaches GA, these agents can bypass registration and deep auditing.

Command-and-Control via AI Providers

Rogue agents may communicate with public LLM APIs over encrypted channels. Because AI providers are often whitelisted, sensitive data exfiltration becomes difficult to detect without behavioral analysis.

Network-Layer Prompt Injection

Prompt injection can occur in transit or through chained agent calls, causing agents to ignore intent or leak sensitive data.

The Current Best Mitigation: Purview Insider Risk Management

Today, the strongest mitigation against insider-driven AI abuse is Microsoft Purview Insider Risk Management (IRM).

  • Behavioral detection beyond static rules
  • Risk scoring across users and agents
  • Unified investigations across Microsoft security tools

Governance Gaps for the Second Line of Defense

Agent 365 primarily serves engineering and platform administrators. Governance teams still rely on Purview for audit and compliance, resulting in fragmented oversight.

Visibility Beyond Microsoft Remains Limited

Native controls struggle with third-party AI tools, non-Microsoft data sources, cross-cloud orchestration, and server-side AI processes.

Licensing and Lock-In Considerations

Advanced AI governance requires E5 licensing, increasing cost pressure and vendor dependency if Microsoft becomes the sole AI registry.

Gartner’s Recommendation: Independent AI TRiSM

Gartner is explicit: no single vendor can enforce runtime control over AI agents once they cross platforms and environments.

  • Define enterprise-wide AI policy
  • Prioritize comprehensive AI discovery
  • Adopt AI TRiSM guardian controls
  • Avoid single-vendor dependency

The Right Architecture: Augment, Don’t Replace

Agent 365 is a step in the right direction, but enterprise AI governance requires vendor-agnostic visibility, cross-cloud enforcement, and independent runtime controls.

Final Thought: Microsoft is building strong first-party controls for AI agents inside its ecosystem. But as AI expands beyond any single vendor, trust, risk, and security must be governed independently.

Organizations that augment Purview and Agent 365 with independent AI TRiSM guardian layers will be best positioned to scale AI securely and with confidence.
